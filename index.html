The Alpha Phi Chapter of Alpha Phi Omega was chartered at Washington University in St. Louis on May 1, 1936. The chapter rechartered October 10, 1981.

About Us

Alpha Phi Omega is a national coeducational service fraternity founded on the cardinal principles of leadership, friendship and service. It provides its members the opportunity to develop leadership skills as they provide service to their campus, to youth and the community, to the nation, and to members of the Fraternity.

Cardinal Principles

The founders insisted that APO members mustly proudly pledge to uphold the fraternity's three cardinal principles of Leadership, Friendship, and Service.


Leadership

Our leadership program comes in many forms. Leading by example, leading by doing, and leading by learning. APO also has variety of leadership development programs.

Friendship

Our social activities, which range from local chapter parties to weekend gatherings involving members from many chapters, provide opportunities to make lasting friendships.

Service

Our projects serve young and old, campus and community, the nation and our own members. members have the opportunity to offer ideas for projects and to see them implemented.
