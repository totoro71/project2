The Alpha Phi Chapter of Alpha Phi Omega was chartered at Washington University in St. Louis on May 1, 1936. The chapter rechartered October 10, 1981.

This is the WashU Alpha Phi Omega chapter and it just gets bigger and better every year!!!

WashU's APO chapter: Alpha Phi

Here are the cardinal principles, explained and interpreted through the eyes of the Alpha Phi chapter here at WashU. As per tradition, the founders insisted that APO members mustly proudly pledge to uphold the fraternity's three cardinal principles of Leadership, Friendship, and Service.


Leadership

Be a leader!

We aim to develop conscientious and capable leaders in our chapter through various interchapter events and workshops.


Off campus events include: National, Regional, and Sectional Conferences and Networking

On campus events include: Leadership Development Day and Safe Zones and Green Dot Training Day.

Friendship

Be a friend!

Fellowship events allow brothers to build a community within their pledge class and the rest of the fraternity.


Off campus events include: St. Louis Balloon Glow, Semiformal & Formal, Ski Trips, City Museum.

On campus events include: Movie nights, S'mores & hot chocolate, DUC lunches

Service

Be of service!

We aim to provide service to the chapter, campus, community and country through a variety of service events


Off campus events include: Food Bank, St. Louis Stay Rescue, Ronald McDonald House.

On campus events include: Girl Scout Day, Sak lunches for Gateway 180, Composting

Risk Management

The Washington Unviersity chapter has a no hazing policy. We are also a dry chapter.
